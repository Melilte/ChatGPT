02 · Definition What is ChatGPT?
AI language model

ChatGPT is a large language model developed by OpenAI. It is a computer program that reads text prompts and generates human-like responses.

  • Trained on large amounts of text in many languages.
  • Can answer questions, explain concepts, and brainstorm ideas.
  • Helps write emails, code, essays, and summaries.

A simple, clear definition helps classmates understand the project.

03 · Technology How ChatGPT Works
Behind the scenes

ChatGPT is based on a transformer neural network. During training, the model learns patterns in language by predicting the next word over and over again.

  • Input: The user types a question or prompt.
  • Processing: The model analyzes the context and past training examples.
  • Output: It predicts and generates the next likely words to form a response.

04 · Numbers Statistics About ChatGPT
Usage & impact

These statistics come from recent reports about how widely ChatGPT is used and what people say about its impact on learning, business, and safety.

Usage & popularity

  • Over 1.8 billion monthly visits.
  • More than 100 million weekly active users.
  • Often described as the fastest-growing consumer app in history.
  • Used by around 80% of Fortune 500 companies.
  • 43% of users say it helps improve their learning.

Safety & ethical concerns

  • About 27% of users report using ChatGPT for emotional support.
  • Studies estimate a 3–10% “hallucination” rate (confident but incorrect answers).
  • Many users sometimes cannot clearly distinguish AI-generated content from human writing.

Education impact

  • Around 61% of students say they use ChatGPT.
  • 43% say it improves their learning experience.
  • Many professors report that student reliance on AI tools is increasing.

05 · Use Case ChatGPT in Education
Schools & learning

ChatGPT can act like a study partner. It can explain homework problems, generate practice questions, and help students brainstorm ideas for writing.

  • Supports language learners with explanations and examples.
  • Gives quick feedback on drafts and outlines.
  • Risk: students may copy answers without understanding, which raises academic integrity concerns.

This connects directly to how you and your classmates use ChatGPT.

06 · Use Case ChatGPT in Health
Well-being

ChatGPT is not a doctor, but it can help explain medical terms, summarize patient education materials, or draft messages for healthcare communication.

  • Can make complex health information easier to understand.
  • Must never replace professional diagnosis or emergency care.
  • Privacy and security of health data are critical concerns.

07 · Ethical Lens 1 Utilitarianism
Greatest good

Utilitarianism judges ChatGPT by asking whether it creates more total happiness than harm. If the benefits for education, work, and accessibility are larger than the risks, a utilitarian might support using it with safeguards.

  • Benefits: faster learning, productivity, support for people with disabilities.
  • Harms: misinformation, job changes, unfair advantages for some students.
  • Goal: maximize benefits while reducing harms through rules, training, and design.

08 · Ethical Lens 2 Virtue Ethics
Character

Virtue ethics focuses on the kind of person we become when we use ChatGPT. It asks whether the technology supports virtues such as honesty, responsibility, and courage.

  • Wise use encourages curiosity, reflection, and life-long learning.
  • Misuse (cheating, copying whole assignments) harms integrity and character.
  • Students, teachers, and designers must model virtuous behavior with AI.

09 · Society Social & Political Concerns
Power & fairness

ChatGPT is not just technical; it is social and political. It raises questions about power, control of data, fairness, and access.

  • Who controls the training data and how transparent is the system?
  • Could AI deepen inequalities between people who have access and those who do not?
  • How should governments, schools, and companies regulate AI to protect rights and democracy?

10 · Looking Ahead Future Recommendations
Our ideas

In this final section we suggest how ChatGPT can be used in a less harmful and more responsible way in the future.

  • Create clear classroom and workplace policies for acceptable AI use.
  • Teach digital literacy so users understand AI limits and biases.
  • Ask AI companies to be transparent about data, safety testing, and updates.
  • Include diverse voices when making rules so AI benefits more people.
