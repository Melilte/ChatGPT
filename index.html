What is ChatGPT?

Definition

ChatGPT is a large language model created by OpenAI. It is a computer program that can understand text prompts and generate human-like responses in many languages.

  • Answers questions and explains concepts.
  • Helps write emails, code, essays, and more.
  • Works by predicting the next likely word in a sentence.

How ChatGPT Works

Technology

ChatGPT is based on a transformer neural network that was trained on large amounts of text. During training, the model learned patterns in language, such as grammar, facts, and writing styles.

  • Input: the user types a prompt or question.
  • Processing: the model analyzes context and patterns.
  • Output: the model predicts and generates the next words.

ChatGPT in Education

Use Case

In education, ChatGPT can act as a study partner, language helper, or writing assistant. It can explain concepts in simple language and give practice questions.

  • Helps students brainstorm ideas and outlines.
  • Supports language learners with grammar and vocabulary.
  • Risk: may encourage plagiarism if students copy answers without thinking.

ChatGPT in Health

Use Case

ChatGPT is not a doctor, but it can support health-related tasks such as explaining medical terms, summarizing patient education materials, or helping schedule and organize information.

  • Can improve communication between patients and providers.
  • Must not replace professional medical advice or diagnosis.
  • Privacy and data protection are very important.

Ethical Lens: Utilitarianism

Ethical Lens 1

Utilitarianism asks whether ChatGPT creates “the greatest good for the greatest number.” From this view, the technology is good if its overall benefits are larger than its harms.

  • Benefits: faster learning, productivity, accessibility.
  • Harms: misinformation, job disruption, unfair advantages in school.
  • Goal: design rules and policies that maximize benefits and minimize harm for society.

Ethical Lens: Virtue Ethics

Ethical Lens 2

Virtue ethics focuses on the kind of person we become when we use ChatGPT. It asks whether the technology supports virtues such as honesty, responsibility, and courage.

  • Using ChatGPT wisely can support curiosity and lifelong learning.
  • Misusing it (cheating, copying work) can harm integrity and character.
  • Teachers and students need clear norms about honest, virtuous use.

Social & Political Concerns

Impacts

ChatGPT raises questions about power, fairness, and control of information. These issues make the technology political as well as technical.

  • Who owns the data used to train AI models?
  • Will AI increase inequality between people who have access to it and those who do not?
  • How should governments and companies regulate AI to protect human rights?

Future Recommendations

Our Ideas

For a less harmful and more helpful future, we recommend combining technical solutions, classroom policies, and public rules.

  • Clear guidelines in schools and workplaces about acceptable AI use.
  • Transparency from companies about how AI systems are trained and limited.
  • Ongoing education so users understand both the power and limits of ChatGPT.
